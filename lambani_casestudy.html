NGAS' Work on Lambani Community

NGAS' Service to the Lambani community in Lingsugur and Maski Taluks of Raichur district , Karnataka

NGAS is working for the development and empowerment of the Lambani community in Lingsugur and Maski taluks of Raichur District, Karnataka. Please find a list of activities that have been taken up for their development.

        

Tuition classes conducted

Tuition classes are being conducted for the students studying in classes 1-7.These classes are held at the Samudaya Bhavan. There were 22 Thandas and 5 Doddis which were covered. Reading, Math, English and Science are taught to them with the support of tutors and volunteers to enable the students to score well in the subjects mentioned. So far 223 girls and 267 boys have been benefited.

        

Formation of Self help Groups

There were 50 SHGs formed covering 750 members. Each SHG has on an average 15 members. In SHG, Accounts training, Minutes writing, Book keeping training are being imparted. On an average, three types of trainings are conducted. Moreover, financial management and leadership training are also imparted to the SHG members. There were 15 SHGs linked to Bank loan @ Rs.2.50 lakh each. Subsidy of Rs.1.25 lakhs also is given.

        

Lambani Dress making training

Experienced women came forward to teach traditional lambani dress making to the youngsters. Teaching handicrafts are also an essential part of this training. This training was provided to 10 thandas. Around 250 women participated. It was held in Samudaya Bhavan for three months from April 2017 to June 2017. As a result of this training now 75 women are involved in preparing lambani dress.

        

Lambani Cultural activities

Once in a month, women gather and songs, language, dance, kunitha, kolata and music steps are taught to the younger generations. Television has had a negative effect on the Lambanis. They seem to have forgotten the lambani culture. The above activities are an effort to keep them connected with their culture. Cultural competitions are also conducted, the best performers are encouraged and prizes given out. There were 500-600 women participating in it.

        

Participated in the Banjara Samaj State and District level meeting and protests to demand reservation and rights

The chief functionary of the NGAS who is also the Organizing Secretary of the Taluk Banjara Samaj Federation and a member of the Banjara Community Federation at the State level participated in the State level struggle to demand reservations and rights. Also participated in the District level meeting.
