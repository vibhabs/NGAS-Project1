Annual report : 2018-2019

Annual report : 2018-2019

(Annual Report period from April 1st 2018 to 31st March, 2019 presented by Ashok Bhati,President ,NGAS)

This year NGAS has been collaborating with KVT – GOK – Vocational Training, Wakf Foundation - Karnataka and GOONJ – Material, Shoes, Clothes, Cloth Pad were distributed to address menstrual hygiene for the women and adolescent girls in the villages and towns. The organization is working for the past 19 years in 78 villages of Lingsugur and Maski Taluks of Raichur District, Hungund Taluk of Bagalkot District, Karnataka. During the reporting period the following activities have been carried out in the organization. I am happy to present to the board. NGAS has 74 Women SHGs having 1116 members, 62 Farmers Group having 1247 members, 24 Youth Groups having 483 members, 35 Children Groups having 629 members. Two Women Federations and one Farmers’ Federation. 2 Child Labour schools running with the support of National Child Labour Project (NCLP). There were 100 children in the centres. One Centre is in Mudgal and another centre is in Lingsugur.

        

Cloth for Work programme

Cloth for work programme was carried out with the support of Goonj, Delhi. It was implemented in Jakkeramadu Babankatti, Ward No.3 & 4 of Neer Doddi, Kadatharahal Thanda, Kadatharahal-Kuntiyar Doddi, Mattur Thanda, Venkatarayanpet, Mudgal and Vandali villages. The village people identify the work on priority basis and work done by the villagers. The works identified are: de-siltation of farm ponds, road cleaning, road laying and widening work, canal cleaning, cleaning of water tank and its surroundings, cleaning of drainage and sanitation, cleaning, mulching, manuring, trimming and watering the plants, check the dam surroundings and stone arrangement works. Instead of wages, cloth was distributed. This has led to recognizing the honour and dignity of the labour. There were 1403 family kits distributed and more than 8,418 family members have benefited.

        

Shoe Distribution

Shoes were distributed in schools and boarding as rewards for projects taken up by them on the topics of importance of cleanliness,conservation of water and environment,and ideas on what they want to contribute to their own village/town when they grow-up.These projects were presented in the form of speech, kit and group works. There were 3432 shoes distributed and 1580 boys and 1852 girls have benefited. These shoes has been distributed as a token of work done by the students. This will honor the student’s work and it has motivated the students to get involved in extra-curricular activities and has improved their socialization and communication skills.

        




Anganwadi Kit and play materials distributed to Anganwadi Centres

This programme was carried out in collaboration with Goonj. 11 Anganwadi Centres have been covered. Play materials and toys have been distributed to 757 children. There were 470 Girls and 287 boys who benefited. This has motivated the Anganwadi teacher first and has led to more children coming to Anganwadi Centre for learning purposes. As a result, drop out rates in the Anganwadi Centre has been reduced considerably and overall hygiene has improved.

        


School Material and school kits distributed

This was done in collaboration with Goonj. School bags and school kits have been given to children from socially and economically deprived sections studying in 8 Govt.schools in and around Lingsugur and Bagalkot Taluks. There were 786 children who benefited [Girls - 370, Boys - 416].

        

Not Just a Piece of Cloth [NJPC] Programme

Under this programme awareness was imparted on menstrual hygiene and cloth pads were distributed and it was also explained how to use the cloth sanitary napkins and how to ensure safety after use and how they could reuse the same cloth. Differences between cloth and plastic pads were explained. This was conducted in collaboration with Goonj. After the session, pads were distributed to 245 women. This has helped the women and adolescent girls to understand their menstrual hygiene and how to keep themselves safe and healthy during their periods. There were 203 women and 121 adolescent girls who were imparted awareness on menstrual hygiene. This has enhanced the understanding of menstrual hygiene and improved their reproductive health.

        

Nutritional Support

NGAS identified dropout children and child labourers in the villages and towns. They have been brought to bridge school. In the bridge school, NGAS has provided nutritional support to 100 children(both girls and boys), since they are deprived of nutritional food in their homes. These children hail from a poor socio-economic background. As a result, the children’s nutritional food intake has improved and they are more focused on their studies. After 12 months of stay, they have been mainstreamed into normal school. 100% of these children were enrolled into Govt schools.Most of the marginalized children are now enrolled in Morarji Desai, Kittur Rani Chennamma Residential School, SC/ST/OBC Hostels. The burden of educational expenses on their parents ,is now gone. These children and staff of these schools have now taken responsibility to report to the Board on any action taken ,as a follow up measure.

        

IEC Materials produced

Information was imparted on the “Swaccha Bharath Campaign". NGAS collaborated with the GP/Municipality/Town Panchayat and this campaign has been implemented in 12 GPs viz, Mattur, Kannal, Hunuru, Baanigolu, Amdihal, Medikinal, Nagalapur, Karadi, Nagarahal, Alkavattigi, Thalikhan, Santhekelluru GPs and 23 wards of Mudgal Municipality. Prevention of Open defection, Drinking water, garbage disposal, conservation of water and environment and how to conserve electricity effectively were some of the topics that were communicated to the village and town people. Support was provided to construct toilet in the houses. There were 500 people who availed of the toilet construction facility from the GPs. As a result, the personal hygiene improved and open defecation is on a downward trend.Awareness on MGNREGA schemes were imparted over a period of 100 days.

        

Street Play and Education on Health

NGAS did street plays based on a health concept (Prevent open defecation and improve sanitation). Tips on disease treatment,communal harmony, promoting social justice, safe drinking water, safe disposal of solid wastage, mosquito control and how to protect oneself from mosquitoes etc have been imparted through street play, drum beating,Yakshakana, folk songs, awareness songs, dance and cultural shows. This has been carried out in collaboration with Mudgal Municipality and it has been done from September 18 to 20th 2018. These street plays have been conducted where the general public gather in one place such as bus station, community hall, circle and the temples. The women gather in community halls, SHG meeting times and the temples. This has been imparted to men, women and children.This message has reached 5,000 people from 23 wards of the Mudgal Municipal area.

        

Rain Water Harvesting Programme

In order to utilize the rain water, a 100% rain water harvesting programme has been organized since this is a drought prone area. Dry land agriculture and crop have been adopted here. In this programme, farmers (male and female)and likeminded youth are included. Successful farmers are invited to deliver address so that the participating farmers can be inspired to take up the rain water harvesting techniques. Topics such as cultivating low water consumption crop and getting more yield ,were taken up. This was conducted from January 13th to 22nd ,2019. There were 118 farmers who participated from 11 villages. As a result, now they are using rain water harvesting techniques and cultivating low water consumption crops and getting more yields.

        

National days and week celebration

In order for the general public,the school children and Office staff to remember the National days ,this was celebrated. The important days being celebrated were: Republic Day, Independence Day, Teacher’s day, Women’s Day, Gandhi Jayanthi, Children’s day, Karnataka Rajyothsava, Ambedkar Jayanthi, Kanakadasha Jayanthi and Valmiki Jayanthi. The purpose of the celebration was to deliver the importance of the day and how it originated.SHG members,youth,school children,teachers,respected elders from the villages and NGAS staff have participated in the celebrations.

        

Beauty Parlour training

There were two batches of training held. 30 members in one batch and in all 120 women have been benefited. After the training certificates have been issued and 60% of the trained women are now employed profitably and 40% of the women are running a beauty parlour shop on their own and they also provide employment to other women in the villages.

        

Shramdana

In order to set and example to the community to contribute to the society by way of small contributions and to demonstrate to the people, NGAS staff has done voluntary services on cleaning, mulching, manuring, trimming and watering plants. This was done in Kadadharal Thanda village.Road laying and widening work in Kadadharahal Kuntiyar Doddi, cleaning the area to support plants and watering the plants in Mudgal town of Lingsugur Taluk was carried out. In all 360 members participated in it. We have asked the people to do this frequently and follow the model that we set and initiated and keep the surroundings neat and tidy so that expense towards health can be reduced considerably.

        

Computer Training and Education Centre

NGAS is registered as a Vocational Training Provider under Government of India, Department of Employment and Training. Vide Reg. No.: 5290600017. Through VTP ,the organization trained 150 members (61 men and 89 women) in Computers. NGAS has 20 computers. It has 4 CC Cameras and 3 Bio-metric devices to monitor them.NGAS has imparted training to students, employed and un-employed youth so that it would enable them to build their skills and after the training they would be able to get better employment opportunities and get a decent salary. NGAS has appointed two computer teachers for the purpose. NGAS have plans to develop this centre as one that offers Information Technology Enabled Services (ITES) to the rural people. Since Raichur District’s literacy rate is 59.63 % as per 2011 Census,we want the educated unemployed youth to become employable after the training. We are looking forward to have some collaborators/donors for the purpose.

        

Staff Member Training

In order to equip the staff, training provided to them. The training focused on topics suc as Global warming, organic farming , alternative sources of energy, communication skills and personality development. Amendment to the Constitution, Article 371 (J) to the Hyderabad Karnataka Region and its implementation were some of teh other topics of trinaing. Translating our weakness into strengths, Identifying public issues and their resolution with the suppor of the public ,were a few other training topics. The resource persons were drawn from NGOs from Bangalore and Mysore districts. There were 10 staff members who benefited.

        

Garments Training

Garments/Tailoring training was provided to un-employed women by NGAS. Under self-employment training, 200 un-employed women have been identified and training imparted. This was a 6 month training. It was carried out from April 2018 to September 2018 and from October to March 2019. After the training certificate was provided and information on bank linkage, garments production and marketing details have been imparted. Now 50 women are doing saree falls, embroidery and stitching blouse and children's dresses. 50 women are employed successfully in nearby areas. As a result, women are supplementing their family income. Rest of the women got married and settled down in their life.

        

Participated in North Karnataka & District level NGO Network meeting

NGAS became part of North Karnataka and District level NGO Network. NGAS participated in North Karnataka NGO network meeting held once in three months and NGAS is a regular participant in NGO Network meeting. It was held in Raichur, Mudgal, Manvi and Sindanur respectively. In that meeting what are the source of funding available to NGOs, how to access them and given guidance for technical support for the same. What are the Government schemes available, what are the formats for that and how to apply them has been imparted. How to mobilize fund for NGOs how to write effective project proposal also imparted for NGOs in the meeting.

        

Swaachha Bharatha Campaign

has been hand over to NGAS by the Mudgal Municipality and it now collects door to door garbage.This has reduced the garbage in the street and it has induced better hygiene among family members. In 23 wards, door to door garbage collection is being done by NGAS. It was supported by the Mudgal Municipality.

        

Sustainable Agricultural Programme

Information was imparted to farmers on climate change, dry land agricultural practices and organic/ natural farming practices. Moreover dry land, sericulture and horticulture crops are also profitable to the farmers.4 programmes were conducted at hobli and GP level. 120 farmers participated in the programme. As a result, the farmers are now aware of climate change and global warming and accordingly now they cultivate crops in their field.

        

Karnataka State Wakf Foundation – Bangalore

Computer and Tailoring training was conducted under this head. There were 75 Muslim women trained in computera and Garments and after the training certificates were distributed to them. As a result, 23% of the trainees are now engaged in small enterprises and rest of them are profitably employed in other works.

        

Participated in Banjara Federation meeting at District and State Level:

A District level meeting was held at Lingsugur on 11th March 2019 and the State level meeting was held at Bangalore on 22nd February 2019. It enabled NGAS to build contacts with the District and State level network organizations and bring pressure on the Govt, for better implementation of development schemes exclusively for Banjara community.

        

NGAS Organized Jatha on waste management, drinking water usage, license for self-employment,stop use of plastics and usage of electricity sparingly:

This programme was organized in collaboration with Mudgal Municipality and local schools organized a jatha on 4th December 2018. It started from Mudgal Municipality office and went through the main streets of Mudgal and imparted awareness to the general public on the above-mentioned subjects and ended in the Mudgal Municipal Office. In that jatha, Municipal Staff, Officials, College, High School, Higher Primary, Primary School students participated. More than 300 persons participated in the jatha. It has enabled NGAS to build more rapport with the Mudgal Municipality.
