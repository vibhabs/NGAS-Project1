Annual report : 2019-2020

Annual report : 2019-2020

(Annual Report period from April 1st 2019 to 31st March, 2020 presented by Ashok Bhati,President ,NGAS)

This year NGAS has been collaborating with KVT – GOK – Vocational Training, Wakf Foundation - Karnataka and GOONJ – Material, Shoes, Clothes, Cloth Pad were distributed to address menstrual hygiene for the women and adolescent girls in the villages and towns. The organization is working for the past 19 years in 78 villages of Lingsugur and Maski Taluks of Raichur District, Hungund Taluk of Bagalkot District, Karnataka. During the reporting period the following activities have been carried out in the organization. I am happy to present to the board. NGAS has 74 Women SHGs having 1116 members, 62 Farmers Group having 1247 members, 24 Youth Groups having 483 members, 35 Children Groups having 629 members. Two Women Federations and one Farmers’ Federation. 2 Child Labour schools running with the support of National Child Labour Project (NCLP). There were 100 children in the centres. One Centre is in Mudgal and another centre is in Lingsugur.

        

Cloth for Work programme

Cloth for work programme was carried out with the support of Goonj, Delhi. It was implemented in Naganur, Harapur, Karadi, Basapur Camp, Hediginbal Camp, Medikinal Karigudda, Medinkinal, Thondihal Ankanal, Gonalu Ambadevi Nagara, Gonalu, Medikinal Thanda, Nanjala Dinni, Hampanal, Paigambar Nagar, and Ankanal – Uppinal villages. The village people identify the work on priority basis and work was done by the villagers. The work identified and implemented are: Samudaya Bhavana surroundings cleaning, bushes cutting, pit digging and plantation, canal cleaning, School ground, Govt. hostel compound cleaning, plant conservation, lake de-siltation, drinking water tank surroundings cleaning, drainage cleaning, plant mulching, fencing and watering the plant. Instead of wage, cloth was distributed. There were 1403 family kit distributed and more than 8,418 family members have been benefited.

        


Shoe Distribution

Shoe distributed in schools and boarding for the reward of projects under taken by them on the topics of importance of cleanliness, what they want to contribute to their own village/town when they grown-up, conservation of water and environment. They have presented in the form of speech, kit and group works. This shoe has been distributed as a token of work done by the students. There were 630 shoes distributed. (Girls – 282, Boys – 348) have been benefited. This will honor the student’s work and it has motivated students to involve in extra-curricular activities and has improved their socialization and communication skills.

        



Cloth Distribution

Cloth distributed to children in child labour school. The children performed what they want to become. There were 110 children benefited.

        




Anganwadi Kit and play materials distributed to Anganwadi Centres

This programme was carried out in collaboration with Goonj organization. There were 21 Anganwadi Centres have been covered. Play materials, toys have been distributed to 1,535 children. There were 638 Girls and 897 boys benefited. This has motivated the Anganwadi teacher first and the children come to Anganwadi Centre for play and learn. As a result, drop out in Anganwadi Centre has been reduced considerably and hygiene improved remarkably.

        


School kits distributed

This was done in collaboration with Goonj organization. School bags, school kits have been distributed to children from socio-economic deprived sections studying in 08 Govt.Schools in an around Lingsugur and Bagalkot Taluks. There were 1079 children benefited. [Girls - 455, Boys - 624].

        


Not Just a Piece of Cloth [NJPC] Programme

Under this programme awareness imparted on menstrual hygiene and cloth pads distributed and also explained how to use cloth sanitary napkins and how to keep safety after use and how they can reuse the same cloth sanitary napkins. Explained what is the difference between cloth and plastic pads. How the plastic pad create negative impact on the environment. This was conducted in collaboration with Goonj organization. After the session dignified pad distributed to 245 women. This has helped the women and adolescent girls to understand their menstrual hygiene and how to keep them safe and healthy during their periods. Apart from this there were 203 women and 121 adolescent girls imparted awareness on menstrual hygiene. This has enhanced the understanding of menstrual hygiene and improved their reproductive health.

        


Nutritional Support

NGAS identified dropout children and child labourers in the villages and towns. They have been brought to bridge school with the consent from the parents. In the bridge school NGAS have provided nutritional support to 100 children both girls and boys, since they are deprived of nutritional food in their homes. This children hails from poor socio-economic status. As a result, the children’s nutritional food intake has been improved and they are concentrating in their studies. After 12 months of stay they have been mainstreamed into normal school. 100% of children enrolled into school. Now they are studying Govt.school and they have improved in their studies. Most of the marginalized children admitted in Morarji Desai, Kittur Rani Chennamma Residential School, SC/ST/OBC Hostels. Hence no burden on the parents for their children’s education. Doing follow up of this, children and staff has been taken responsibility and report to the Board for the action taken.

        

IEC Materials produced

Information imparted on “Swaccha Bharath Campaign’’. NGAS collaborated with the GP/Municipality/Town Panchayat and it has been implemented in 7 GPs viz, Mattur, Kannal, Hunuru, Baanigolu, Amdihal, Medikinal, Nagalapur GPs and 23 wards of Mudgal Municipality. Drinking water, garbage disposal, conservation of water and environment, how to use the electricity effectively communicated to the village and town people. More over awareness imparted on 100 days MGNREGA schemes.

        


Rain Water Harvesting Programme

In order to utilize the rain water 100% rain water harvesting programme have been organized since this is the drought prone area. Dry land agriculture and crop have been adopted here. In this programme farmers (male and female), likeminded youths are included. In that meeting the successful farmers are invited to deliver address so that they can be inspired and used the rain water harvesting technique to them. He also addressed how to cultivate low water consumption crop and get more yield. This was conducted from January 13 to 22, 2020. There were 120 farmers have been participated from 11 villages. As a result, now they are using rain water harvesting techniques and cultivating low water consumption crops and getting more yields.

        

National important days celebration

The important days being celebrated were: Republic Day, Independence Day, Teacher’s day, Women’s Day, Gandhi Jayanthi, Children’s day, Karnataka Rajyothsava, Ambedkar Jayanthi, Kanakadasha Jayanthi and Valmiki Jayanthi. The purpose of celebration was that to deliver the importance of the day and in what circumstance we have got this day and keep them in national main stream. The SHG members, youths, school children, teachers, respected elders from the villages and NGAS staff have been participated in the celebration.

        


Beauty Parlour training

There were seven batches of training held. 30 members each per batch and in all 210 women have been benefited. After the training certificate issued and 60% of women employed profitably and 40% of women running beauty parlour shop on their own and they also provide employment to other women in the villages.

        

Shramadana

In order to show to the community to contribute to the society by way of small contribution. To demonstrate the people, NGAS staff has done voluntary services to cleaning, mulching, manuring, trimming and watering the plant. This was done in Kadadharahal Thanda village, Road laying and widening of work in Kadadharahal Kuntiyar Doddi, cleaning the area to support plants and watering the plants in Mudgal town of Lingsugur Taluk. In all 360 members were participated in it. We have asked the people to do it frequently as NGAS done as a model. Have to continue the model work NGAS have been initiated and keep the surroundings neat and tidy so that expense towards health can be reduced considerably.

        

Computer Training and Education Centre

NGAS have registered under Vocational Training Provider under Government of India, Department of Employment and Training. Vide Reg. No.: 5290600017. Through VTP the organization trained 200 members of which 89 men and 111 women in Computer. NGAS has 20 computers, 4 CC Camera and 3 Bio-metric devices to monitor them. NGAS imparted training to the students, employed and un-employed youths. So that it would enable them to build their skills and after the training they can able to get better employment opportunity and get decent salary. NGAS have appointed two computer teachers for the purpose. NGAS have plans to develop this centre as Information Technology Enabled Services (ITES) to the rural people. Since Raichur District’s literacy rate is 59.63 % as per 2011 Census. We want the educated unemployed youth becomes employable after the training. We are looking forward to have some collaborators/ donors for the purpose.

        

Staff Member Training

In order to equip the staff, training provided to them. The training focused on Global warming, climate change, organic farming and alternative source of energy, communication skills and personality development. Amendment to the Constitution, Article 271 (J) to the Hyderabad Karnataka Region and how to implement the same. How to translate our weakness into strength, how to identify the issues in the public and how to address with the support of the general public. The resource persons were drawn from NGOs from Raichur and Bangalore districts. There were 10 staff have been benefited.

        

Garments Training

Tailoring training provided to un-employed women by the NGAS on its own source. Under self-employment training, 120 un-employed women have been identified and training imparted. This was 6 months training. It was carried out from April 2019 to September 2019 and from October to March 2020. After the training certificate provided and imparted information on bank linkage, garments production and marketing details have been imparted. Now 60 women are doing saree falls, embroidery and stitching blouse and children dress. 40 women employed successfully nearby areas. As a result, women are supplementing their family income. Rest of the women got married and settled in their life.

        

Participated in North Karnataka & District level NGO Network meeting

NGAS became part of North Karnataka and District level NGO Network. NGAS participated in North Karnataka NGO network meeting held once in three months and NGAS is regular participant in NGO Network meeting. It was held in Raichur, Mudgal, Manvi and Sindanur respectively. NGAS participated District level NGO network meeting periodically. Once in a quarter (once in 3 months) on 5th day of the third month meeting have been fixed. In that meeting what were the source of funding available to NGOs, how to access them and given guidance for technical support for the same. What are the Government schemes available, what are the formats for that and how to apply them have been imparted. How to mobilize fund for NGOs how to write effective project proposal also imparted for NGOs in the meeting.

        

Participated in PACNI National level Meet

NGAS participated in PACNI National level meeting held at Pastoral Centre, Bhopal on 7th and 8th Feb 2020. Presented the Nomads work in Karnataka and prepared plan of action and presented the same in the meeting. This has enabled NGAS to built network among the different State NGOs so that issues being address at the National level. Through this NGAS spread its wings at the National level.

        

TMC: “Swaachha Bharatha Campaign’’

Has been hand over to NGAS by the Mudgal Municipality and it collects door to door garbage and it has reduced the garbage in the street and it has induced the hygiene among the family members. In 23 wards door to door garbage collection done by NGAS. It was supported by the Mudgal Municipality.

        

Sustainable Agricultural Programme

Information imparted to farmers on climate change, dry land agricultural practices, organic/ natural farming practices. More over dry land, sericulture and horticulture crops also profitable to the farmers. It was conducted in hobli, GP level and 4 programmes conducted. There were 128 farmers have been participated. As a result, the farmers aware of the climate change and global warming and accordingly they cultivate crops in their field.

        

Participated in Banjara Federation meeting at District and State Level

NGAS participated District level meeting held at Belgaum on 15th February 2020 and the State level meeting held at Bangalore on 29th February 2020. It enabled the NGAS to built contact with the District and State level network organizations and bring pressure on the Govt. for better implementation of development schemes exclusively for Banjara community.

        

Participated in Goonj State level Anniversary meeting

NGAS participated in Goonj State level anniversary meeting held at Azim Premji Foundation on 16th Feb 2020.

        

Participated in GAP Training in Bengaluru and Ahmedabad

NGAS Chief Functionary Mr. Ashok Bhati participated in GAP Training which was held at Bengaluru from 17th September to 20th September 2019 and also in Ahmedabad from 14th November to 15th November 2019. It has enabled the Chief Functionary in picking up communication skills and came to know the various resource organizations throughout the country and fund mobilization efforts for the sustainability of the organization.
