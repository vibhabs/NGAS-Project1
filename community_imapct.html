Community Impact

How NGAS has impacted the community around it

  • NGAS imparted awareness on dry land agriculture, soil and water conservation techniques, kitchen garden concept, organic farming practices, preparation of Panchagavya and Jeevamirtha to implement in the farmers land. 1212 farmers have been benefited out of it.

  • De-siltation of Check dams and the silt has been applied in the field as a result the fertility of the soil has improved. 33 check dams have been de-silted and 1862 farmers have been benefited. As a result, the ground water table has been enhanced, resulting in water availability for cattle and the green cover being enhanced.

  • NGAS changed its approach from charity to Right Based (RB) from 2006. The Right to Information Act, Right to Education Act, Right to Employment (MGNREGA) Act, Right to Food Act, Juvenile Justice Act [JJ Act), Forest Right Act, Karnataka Land Right Act have been imparted to the community. As a result, 2056 members have been benefited.

  • Handling of solid and dry waste, sanitation, health and good drinking water, environmental aspects, benefits of saving electricity have been imparted to the general public with the help from Mudgal Municipality. It was done in 19 wards of Mudgal Municipality area. 7000 people have been benefited. In order to impart awareness, the organization has been adopting various methods such as street play, hand bills, wall writing, banners and drums beating.

  • Child labour and housing survey has been conducted with the help of District Child Labour Authority and Mudgal Municipality and Gram Panchayat. 2004 child labour surveys has been conducted and 15000 housing surveys conducted and the report submitted to the concerned Govt. authorities.
