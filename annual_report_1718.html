Annual report : 2017-2018

Annual report : 2017-2018

(Annual Report period from April 1st 2017 to 31st March, 2018 presented by Ashok Bhati,President ,NGAS)

This year NGAS has been collaborating with KVT – GOK – Vocational Training and GOONJ – Material, Shoes, Clothes, Cloth Pad distribution to address menstrual hygiene for the women and adolescent girls in the villages and towns. The organization is working for the past 18 years and in 68 villages of Lingsugur and Maski Taluks of Raichur District, Hungund Taluk of Bagalkot District, Karnataka. During the reporting period the following activities have been carried out in the organization.

        

Anganwadi Kit, Shoe and play materials distributed to Anganwadi Centres

This programme was carried out in collaboration with Goonj organization. There were 25 Anganwadi centres that were covered. Shoe and play materials were distributed to 648 children. There were 356 Girls and 292 boys who benefited. This has motivated the anganwadi teacher first and the children who come to the anganwadi centre to play and learn. As a result, drop outs in Anganwadi centre has been reduced considerably and hygiene improved.

        

School Material and kits distributed

This was done in collaboration with Goonj organization. School bags & school kits have been given to children from socio-economically deprived sections of the society ,studying in 30 Govt. schools in an around Lingsugur and Bagalkot Taluks. There were 2032 children who benefited. [Girls - 1209, Boys - 823].

        

Cloth for Work Programme and shoe distribution

Cloth for work programme was carried out with the support of Goonj, Delhi. It was implemented in Ramatnal, Kadatharahall, Kadatharahall Thanda, Krishnapur, Talekattu, Adapur, Ankanal, Vandli and Mattur villages. The village people identify the work on priority basis and work is done by the villagers. Instead of wages, cloth is distributed. This has honour and dignity of the labour. There were 1605 family kits distributed and more than 10,000 family members who have benefited from this.
Shoes were distributed in schools and boarding. There were 7700 shoes distributed. Girls – 4620, Boys – 3080 have been benefited. Shoes has been distributed as a token of work done by the students. This will honor the student’s work and it has motivated students to involve in extra curricular activities and has improved their socialization and communication skills.

        




Not Just a Piece of Cloth [NJPC] Programme

This was conducted in collaboration with Goonj organization. This has helped the teenage girls to understand their menstrual hygiene and how to keep themselves safe and healthy during their periods. There were 350 adolescent girls who have benefited. This has enhanced the understanding of menstrual hygiene and improved their reproductive health.

        


Nutritional Support

NGAS has identified school dropout children in the villages. They have been brought to the bridge school. In the bridge school, NGAS has provided nutritional support to 100 children, both girls and boys, since they are deprived of nutritional food in their homes. These children hails from a poor socio-economic background. As a result, the children’s nutritional food intake has been improved and they focus on their studies. After 12 months of stay, they have been mainstreamed into normal school. All the children have now been enrolled into government school and are doing better at their studies.

        

IEC Materials produced

Information is power. In order to improve the information for the general public the relevant materials have been produced and distributed to them through hand bills, banners and it has reached every household through local daily news papers. Information was imparted on the “Swacch Bharath Camapign’’. Organisation collaborated with the GP and it has been implemented in 7 GPs viz, Mattur, Kannal, Hunuru, Baanigolu, Amdihal, Medikinal, Nagalapur GPs and 23 wards of Mudgal Municipality. Tips on clean Drinking water, garbage disposal ,conservation of water and environment, how to use the electricity effectively etc were communicated to the village and town people. Support was provided to construct toilets in the houses. There were 350 people who availed of the toilet construction facility. As a result, the personal hygiene improved and open defecation is on a down trend. More over awareness was imparted on 100 days MGNREGA objectives and its purpose.

        

EDP Training

Phenol and soap making training imparted to 180 women. As a result, the 60 women started their own micro enterprises and their family income has increased.

        

Street Play on Health

NGAS did street play on health concept (Prevent open defecation and improve sanitation). Tips on disease treatment,communal harmony, promoting social justice, safe drinking water, safe disposal of solid wastage, how to control mosquitoes and how to protect oneself from mosquitoes etc have been imparted through street play, drum beating,Yakshakana, folk song, awareness song, dance and cultural shows. This has reached a population of 5000 from 16 wards of Mudgal Municipal area. This has been carried out in collaboration with Mudgal Municipality and it has been done from August 13 to 20th 2017. These street plays have been conducted where the general public gather in one place such as bus station, community hall, circle and the temples. The women gather in community halls, SHG meeting times and the temples. This has been imparted to men, women and children.

        

Rain Water Harvesting Programme

In order to utilize the rain water 100% rain water harvesting programme have been organized since this is a drought prone area. Dry land agriculture and crops have been adopted here. In this programme farmer (male and female) & likeminded youth are included. In these meetings, the successful farmers are invited to deliver address so that others can be inspired and used the rain water harvesting technique. The topic of how to cultivate low water consumption crops and get more yield was also addressed. This was conducted from January 15 to 25, 2017. There were 110 farmers who have participated ,from 12 villages. As a result, now they are using rain water harvesting techniques and cultivating low water consumption crops and getting more yield.

        

National days and week celebration

In order to remember the National days among the general public and office staff this was celebrated. The important days being celebrated are the Republic Day, the Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanthi, the Teacher’s day, Children’s day, Karnataka Rajyothsava, Ambedkar Jayanthi, Kanakadasha Jayanthi and Valmiki Jayanthi. The purpose of celebrations was to deliver the importance of the day and in what circumstances the day got created. SHG members, youth, school children, teachers, respected elders from the village have been the participants in the celebration.

        


Beauty Parlour training

There were two batches of training held. 30 members per batch and in all 120 women have benefited. After the training certificate have been issued and 60% of women employed profitably and 40% of women running beauty parlours on their own and are also providing employment to other women in the villages.

        

Shramdana

In order to show the community how to contribute to the society in simple ways, NGAS staff have done voluntary services to clean the dhobi ghat,streets, cleaning of garbage and drainage for model purposes and the ward where they teamed up with the general public. This was done in 3 villages of Lingsugur Taluk. In all 260 members participated in it. As a result, Dhobi Ghat,street and school ground now looks cleaner,and health and sanitation conditions have improved in their respective wards. We have encouraged the people to keep up the cleaning work on a regular basis in order to reduce expenses towards maintaining their personal health.

        

Computer Training and Education Centre

NGAS has registered as a Vocational Training Provider under Government of India, Department of Employment and Training, Vide Reg. No.: 529060017. Through VTP the organization trained 240 members of which 96 were men and the rest women.NGAS has 20 computers of its own and has imparted training to the students, employed and un-employed youth to enable them to build their skills and procure better employment oppurtunities.NGAS has appointed one computer teacher for the purpose. NGAS have plans to develop this center as Information Technology Enabled Services (ITES) center to serve the rural people. We are looking forward to having some collaborators/ donors for the purpose.

        

Staff Member Training

In order to equip the staff, training is provided to them. The training focused on global warming, organic farming and alternative sources of energy, communication skills and personality development. Some of the topics that were addressed were ways of Translating one's weakness into a strength, identifying the issues that affect the general public and how to address with the support of the general public. The resource persons were drawn from NGOs from Bangalore and Mysore districts. There were 10 staff who have been benefited.

        


Garments Training

Tailoring training is provided to un-employed women by NGAS on its own source. Under self-employment training, 60 un-employed women have been identified and training imparted. This was a 6 month training. It was carried out from August 2017 to January 2018. After the training, certificates were provided and information on bank loans, garments production and marketing have been imparted. Now 50 women are doing saree falls, embroidery and stitching blouse and children dresses. As a result, women are supplementing their family income.

        

Participated in State level NGO Federation meeting

NGAS became member of various State level networks such as CACL-K, CACT-K, and Right to Food Network etc. NGAS participated in State level NGO Federation meeting which was held on November 19, 2017 at Tumkur University. In that meeting FCRA, Local contribution, linking with various line Govt. departments, various Acts related to NGOs and FCRA were discussed. The Federation sends all information to member organizations periodically about the funds available at the Govt, CSR projects.

        

Participated in North Karnataka & District level NGO Network meeting

NGAS became part of North Karnataka and District level NGO Network. NGAS participated in North Karnataka NGO network meeting that is held once in three months and NGAS is a regular participant in this meeting. It was held in Raichur, Mudgal, Manvi and Sindanur for the year 2017-18. The District level NGO network meeting is held once in three months on the 5th day of the third month. The topics discussed in that meeting include the sources of funding available to NGOs, how to access them and provide guidance & technical support for the same. Knowledge on the Government schemes available, the formats for the schemes and how to apply to them have been imparted. Apart from these topics like How to mobilize funds for NGOs, how to write effective project proposals are also imparted for NGOs.

        

Swaachha Bharatha Campaign

This campaign has been handed over to NGAS and it collects door to door garbage and it has reduced the garbage in the street and has induced better hygiene among the family members. It was supported by the Mudgal Town Panchayat.In all 23 wards ,door to door garbage collection is done by NGAS.

        

Sustainable Agricultural Programme

Information was imparted to farmers on climate change, dry land agricultural practices, organic/ natural farming practices. Moreover dry land agriculture,sericulture and horticulture crops are also profitable to the farmers. Four programs were conducted in hobli and GP level. There were 1s20 farmers who participated. As a result, the farmers are now aware of the climate change and global warming and accordingly they cultivate crops in their field.

        

Karnataka State Wakf Foundation – Bangalore

Computer and Tailoring training conducted were under this head. There were 75 muslim women members trained and after the training, certificates distributed to them. As a result, 23% of the trainees are now engaged in small enterprises and rest of them profitably employed in other works.

        

PACNI -INDIA Programme

NGAS has participated in Pastrol Care for Nomads India (PACNI) – National meet held at Bhopal on 11th and 12th March 2018 which was conducted by PACNI National wing. Have worked out action plan for Karnataka and also presented Karnataka case for National wing support. The President and Project Coordinator of NGAS have attended the meeting. It has enabled NGAS to spread its wings to the National level for networking and policy making at National level for the development of Nomads community.

        


Participated in Banjara Federation meeting at District and State Level

District level meeting held at Maski on 4th March 2018 and the State level meeting held at Bangalore on 15th February 2018. It enabled NGAS to build contact with the District and State level network organizations and bring pressure on the Govt. for better implementation of development schemes exclusively for the Banjara community.

        

Participated in State level Seminar on CSR: Opportunities and Challenges for NGOs and Corporate

This seminar was jointly organized by Institute for Youth and Development (IYD) and Department of studies and Research in Social Work, Tumkur University with the support from Vishwa Yuvak Kendra (VYK), New Delhi. It was held on 18th and 19th November 2017 at the Tumkur University Auditorium. It has enabled NGAS to build rapport and contact with CSR companies for resource mobilization.

        

Participated in Goonj 2 decades celebration in Bangalore

NGAS is one of the partner organizations of Goonj. Goonj celebrated its 3 decades of completion of service in India and it was held in Bangalore on 7th March 2018. NGAS and its team members have been part of the programme.

        

GAP Team visited for feasibility funding to NGAS

Global Action on Poverty [GAP] team members visited NGAS on 3rd February 2018 for assessment and see the field reality of NGAS skill building carried out for the past 5 years. The team had visited all skill building centres and had interaction with various stakeholders to assess the impact of the programme and also venture out various activities for funding. The visit enabled the team members to understand the NGAS work and enable resource mobilization for NGAS.

We are received Rs.3,17,385/- from Klaas Oskam ,Managing Director of Signal Hill Organisation through GAP. From this amount NGAS purchased 9 computers and installed the same for training of more number of candidates in Mudgal Computer Training Centre. NGAS acknowledges with gratitude the donation made by the Signal Hill Organisation through GAP.
