Annual report : 2016-2017

Annual report : 2016-2017

(Annual Report period from April 1st 2016 to 31st March, 2017 presented by Ashok Bhati,President ,NGAS)

This year NGAS collaborated with Karnataka Vocational training institute (Government of Karnataka) and GOONJ – for Material, Shoes, Clothes, Cloth Pad distribution to address menstrual hygiene for the women and adolescent girls in the villages and towns.
The organization is working for the past 16 years and in 55 villages of Lingsugur Taluk, Mudgal Hobli, Raichur District, Hungund Taluk, Bagalkot District, Karnataka. During the reporting period the following activities have been carried out in the organization. I am happy to present the below to the board.

        

School bags and school kits distributed

This was done in collaboration with Goonj. School bags, school kits have been given to children from the deprived sections of the society, studying in 30 Government schools in an around Lingsugur and Bagalkot Taluks. There were 2032 children who benefited. [Girls - 1209, Boys - 823].

        

Anganwadi Kit, Shoe and play materials distributed to Anganwadi centres

This programme was carried out in collaboration with Goonj. There were 25 Anganwadi centers that were covered. 15 Anganwadi kits were distributed. Shoe and play materials were distributed to 648 children. There were 356 Girls and 292 boys who benefited. This has motivated the Anganwadi teacher first and also the children who come to Anganwadi centers to play and learn. As a result, dropout rates in the Anganwadi centers have been reduced considerably.

        

Cloth for Work Programme and shoe distribution

Cloth for work programme was carried out with the support of Goonj, Delhi. It was implemented in Ramatnal, Kadatharahall, Kadatharahall Thanda, Krishnapur, Talekattu, Adapur, Ankinal, Wonthali and Mattur villages. The village people identify the work that needs to be done and the work is carried out by the villagers. Instead of wages, cloth is distributed. This is a novel way of honouring the labour. There were 1605 family kits that were distributed and more than 10,000 family members who benefited.
Shoe distribution was carried out in schools. There were 7700 shoes distributed. Girls – 4620, Boys – 3080 have been benefited. These shoes were distributed as a token of work done by the students. This will honour the student’s work and it has motivated the students to involve themselves in extracurricular activities and this in turn has improved their socialization and communication skills.

        




Not Just a Piece of Cloth [NJPC] Programme

This has helped the teenage girls to understand their menstrual hygiene and how to keep them safe and healthy during their periods. There were 700 adolescent girls and 300 women who have benefited. This programme has been helpful and they have been provided access to low cost sanitary pads. There were 1000 cloth pads distributed to the SHG women and adolescent girls studying in High Schools and Colleges. This has enhanced the understanding of menstrual hygiene and improved their reproductive health

        


Nutritional Support

NGAS has identified school dropout children in the villages. They have been brought to the bridge school. In the bridge school, NGAS has provided nutritional support to 100 children, both girls and boys, since they are deprived of nutritional food in their homes. These children hails from a poor socio-economic background. As a result, the children’s nutritional food intake has been improved and they focus on their studies. After 12 months of stay, they have been mainstreamed into normal school. All the children have now been enrolled into government school and are doing better at their studies.

        

IEC Materials produced

Information is power. In order to improve the information for the general public the following materials have been produced and distributed to them through hand bills, banner and reached every household through local daily newspaper. Information was imparted on “Swacch Bharath Camapign’’. Organization collaborated with the GP and it has been implemented in Mattur, Kannal, Hunuru, Baanigolu and Amdihal GPs and 19 wards of Mudgal Town Panchayat. Support was provided to construct toilets in the houses. There were 320 people who availed of the toilet construction facility. As a result, the personal hygiene improved and open defecation in on a downtrend. Moreover awareness was imparted on 100 days on MGNREGA objectives and their purpose.

        

Street Play Education on Health

NGAS organized street plays on health concepts (Prevent open defecation and improve sanitation). Concept of visiting a doctor , providing safe drinking water, safe disposal of solid waste, controlling mosqitoes,protecting oneself from mosquitoes have been imparted through street plays, drum beating,Yakshakana, folk songs, dance and cultural shows. This has reached 5,000 people from 10 wards, in the Mudgal Municipal area. This has been carried out in collaboration with Mudgal Municipality and it has been done from February 15 - 25, 2017. The street plays have been conducted where the general public normally gather in numbers like the bus station, community hall, circles and the temples. The women mostly gather in community halls, SHG meetings and in the temples.

        

Rain Water Harvesting Programme

In order to utilize the rain water, rain water harvesting programme have been organized since this is a drought prone area. Dry land agriculture and suitable crops have been adopted here. In this programme farmer (male and female) & likeminded youth are included. In these meetings the successful farmers are invited to talk about rain water harvesting techniques and cultivation of low water consumption crops and also techniques to raise the yield. This was conducted from January 15 to 25, 2017. There were 110 farmers who have participated from 12 villages. As a result, now they are using rain water harvesting techniques and cultivating low water consumption crops and getting more yield.

        

National days and week celebration

In order to remember the National days among the general public and office staff this was celebrated. The important days being celebrated are the Republic Day, the Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanthi, the Teacher’s day, Children’s day, Karnataka Rajyothsava, Ambedkar Jayanthi, Kanakadasha Jayanthi and Valmiki Jayanthi. The purpose of celebrations was to deliver the importance of the day and in what circumstances the day got created. SHG members, youth, school children, teachers, respected elders from the village have been the stakeholders of the celebration.

        

Beauty Parlour training

There were two batches of training held. 20 members per batch and in all 40 women have benefited. After the training certificate have been issued and 60% of women employed profitably and 40% of women running beauty parlours on their own and are also providing employment to other women in the villages.

        

Shramdana

In order to show the community how to contribute to the society in simple ways, NGAS staff have done voluntary services to clean the streets, cleaning of garbage and drainage for model purposes and the ward where they teamed up with the general public. This was done in 2 villages of Lingsugur Taluk. In all 210 members participated in it. As a result, health and sanitation conditions have improved in their respective wards. We have encouraged the people to keep up the cleaning work on a regular basis in order to reduce expenses towards maintaining their personal health.

        

Computer Training and Education Centre

NGAS has registered as a Vocational Training Provider under Government of India, Department of Employment and Training, Vide Reg. No.: 529060017. Through VTP the organization trained 100 members of which 45 were men and the rest women.NGAS has 20 computers of its own and has imparted training to the students, employed and un-employed youth to enable them to build their skills and procure better employment oppurtunities.There were 250 persons (Women 180+ Men 70) who have benefited within a span of one year. NGAS has appointed two computer teachers for the purpose. NGAS have plans to develop this center as Information Technology Enabled Services (ITES) center to serve the rural people. We are looking forward to having some collaborators/ donors for the purpose.

        

Staff Member Training

In order to equip the staff, training is provided to them. The training focused on global warming, organic farming and alternative sources of energy, communication skills and personality development. translating one's weakness into a strength, how to identify the issues that affect the general public and how to address with the support of the general public. The resource persons were drawn from NGOs from Bangalore and Mysore districts. There were 10 staff who have been benefited. Apart from that staff also attended training which was held in Bangalore.

        

Garments Training

Tailoring training is provided to un-employed women by NGAS on its own source. Under self-employment training, 60 un-employed women have been identified and training imparted. This was a 6 month training. It was carried out from August 2016 to January 2017. After the training, certificates were provided and information on bank loans, garments production and marketing have been imparted. Now 50 women are doing saree falls, embroidery and stitching blouse and children dress. As a result, women are supplementing their family income.

        

Participated in State level NGO Federation meeting

NGAS became member of various State level networks such as CACL-K, CACT-K, and Right to Food Network etc. NGAS participated in State level NGO Federation meeting which was held on December 10, 2016 at ISI, Bangalore. In that meeting FCRA, Local contribution, linking with various line Govt. departments, various Acts related to NGOs and FCRA were discussed. State level NGO Federation has been formed and it will meet frequently and discuss how to involve NGOs in Government schemes implementation effectively. It was decided to provide information to all NGOs by advertising in local dailies so that every NGOs can apply for the same for the support from the Government.

        

Participated in North Karnataka & District level NGO Network meeting

NGAS became part of North Karnataka and District level NGO Network. NGAS participated in North Karnataka NGO network meeting that is held once in three months and NGAS is a regular participant in this meeting. It was held in Raichur, Mudgal, Manvi and Sindanur for the year 2016-17. The District level NGO network meeting is held once in three months on the 5th day of the third month. The topics discussed in that meeting include the sources of funding available to NGOs, how to access them and provide guidance & technical support for the same. Knowledge on the Government schemes available, the formats for the schemes and how to apply to them have been imparted. Apart from these topics like How to mobilize funds for NGOs, how to write effective project proposals are also imparted for NGOs.
NGAS called for a Raichur and Bellary Districts NGO Network meeting on 13/02/2017 at the NGAS Office, Mudgal to identify the network NGOs who would work for the CFW Programme for GOONJ.

        

Swaachha Bharatha Campaign

This campaign has been handed over to NGAS and it collects door to door garbage and it has reduced the garbage in the street and has induced better hygiene among the family members. It was supported by the Mudgal Town Panchayat.

        

Sustainable Agricultural Programme

Information was imparted to farmers on climate change, dry land agricultural practices, organic/ natural farming practices. Moreover dry land agriculture,sericulture and horticulture crops are also profitable to the farmers. Three programs were conducted in hobli and GP level. There were 220 farmers who participated. As a result, the farmers are now aware of the climate change and global warming and accordingly they cultivate crops in their field.

        

Karnataka State Wakf Foundation – Bangalore

Computer and Tailoring training conducted were under this head. There were 100 women members trained and after the training, certificates distributed to them. As a result, 20% of the trainees are now engaged in small enterprises and rest of them profitably employed in other works.

        

PACNI -SOUTH INDIA Programme

NGAS has participated in Pastoral Care for Nomads India (PACNI) - South India programme which was organized by TENT Society and PACNI - India jointly and it was held from 29 to 30th November 2016 at Madurai. It has enabled NGAS to spread its wings to other parts of South India and the National level for the development of Nomadic community.
